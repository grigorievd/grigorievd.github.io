OKAYAMA ART SUMMIT

Trithagorean Hoga
— Paul Chan
In another example of creativity powering rebirth, redevelopment is at work thanks to the small-scale art festivals proliferating across the city of Okayama.

North of Hiroshima, Okayama embodies the spirit of Japan's traditions. Its old-style streets are reminiscent of an older Japan, provoking feelings of nostalgia for visitors to the historic landmarks and picturesque landscape. Okayama also boasts a mild climate that makes it the perfect home for high-quality fruits such as white peaches and muscat grapes.

First held in Okayama City in 2016, the summit takes place every three years. It is an international exhibition of contemporary art aimed at social engagement and revival. One of the festival’s most incredible locations is the Okayama Castle, where artwork is displayed among ancient culture.

Belgian film artist Eva L’Hoest’s “Under Automata” makes use of the Fukuoka Soy Sauce Factory’s 19th century architecture as an exhibition stage while French conceptual artist Pierre Huyghe showcases his “Not yet titled” at the and the Uchisange Elementary School.

Resident’s Story

Since its debut, the summit has brought in an influx of visitors and local resident KURODA Koichi saw the opportunity to let visitors appreciate the region even more. He started the Okayama Kyobashi Cruise which connects Kyobashi, located in the central area of Okayama City, to Inujima and Ushimado Port.

“50 years ago, there were many ships traveling in and out of Kyobashi. We wanted to restore the route that was once a port of call. I started Kyobashi cruise because I wanted to bring activity back to this port. I wanted to make sure visitors could stop by Okayama to visit the city,” shares Mr Kuroda.

With this gateway, visitors are able to spend some time in Okayama City before heading to the Setouchi Triennale. Visitors to Inujima island, a small island off Okayama, will be able to not only enjoy modern art on display but they can also rent condos for their stay on the island and enjoy scenic meals at cafes operated by the locals who live in this small seaside village.
