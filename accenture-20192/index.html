Cyber
Resilience

Cyber resilience is often equated with a “bend but don’t break” attitude. Nowhere is it clearer than in enterprises trying to innovate and grow simultaneously within a fragile digital landscape. Doing this successfully demands not just forging digital trust but also ensuring a solid cybersecurity foundation. However, enterprise security among businesses, especially in India, leaves a lot to be desired.

Cyber Security:
The focus of preventing/ protecting business organization networks against cyber attacks and/or events (unauthorized access, network integrity).

Cyber Resilience (CR):
The ability for an organization to quickly recover and deliver the intended results even when affected by cyber events and/or attacks that disrupt their day to day business/operations.

A Case Study
of the Indian
Scenario

Despite the regulatory norms in place, cybercrime reported by NCRB rose from 9,622 to 11,592 in 2014-15.

Certain sectors of Indian industry are at the forefront of data breaches – Information technology (IT) and IT-enabled services (ITES) (40%), manufacturing (17.88%), education (12.56%), and hospitality (9.17%).

DETECTIONS PER SECOND

CERT-In reported phishing, scanning/probing, website intrusions/defacements, ransomware, denial of service attacks, use of potentially unwanted applications (PUAs) and adware, infectors, worms and cryptojacking etc. amounting to 27,482 cybersecurity incidents in June, 2017.

But while ransomware ruled the roost in 2017 with 28% of malware attacks, cryptominers constituted 9% of the threats. In 2018, ransomware accounted for 13% of malware attacks and cryptojackers rose to 27%.

India was thus placed second only to the US with 37% of global data breaches where “information was stolen, compromised or revealed”.

Some of the most controversial cybersecurity breaches last year included attacks on India’s Aadhar and Quora, which makes it critical to classify risks and understand them better.

In the first half of 2017 alone, at least one cyber attack was reported every 10 minutes. Totalling 27,482 reported cases in just 6 months. These include phishing, scanning or probing, site intrusions, defacements, virus or malicious code, ransomware (bitcoin), and denial-of-service attacks.

In just the past three and a half years since the report, India has seen a total of 171,000 cases of cyber crimes.

Categorizing Risks

IT/technology risks

These include but are not limited to data thefts, compromised accounts, lost files and disabled systems, obsolete legacy systems. In addition, enterprises may face risks related to – IT strategy, cyber security and incident response, IT resilience and business continuity, vendors and third-parties, data management, IT program execution, technology operations and ineffective risk management.

Among these, cybersecurity (31%) and disruptive technologies (25%) pose big risks to Indian businesses though the trend is expected to shift by 2021, with cybersecurity taking the lead with 36% followed by technology disruption risk at 33%.

However, the picture is not as bleak as it seems. Accenture’s 2018 State of Cyber Resilience Report maintains that only 1 in 8 targeted cybersecurity attacks got through in 2018 which is a comforting statistic. 83% of their survey respondents maintained that emergent tech like AI, ML, IoT, Blockchain actually enhanced cybersecurity.

While 87% of security breaches were prevented, enterprises only faced 2-3 breaches a month. 90% of respondents felt investments in cybersecurity would rise over the next 3 years but 31 percent were sure these would double. 71% respondents maintained they were unsure of when and how attacks would impact enterprises but 72% stated they would capitulate if security was breached.

Warding off cyberattacks and protecting data then, is possible when enterprises recognise that developing cyber resilience is more a “boardroom imperative” than just an “IT issue” and up the ante for it enterprise-wide.

5 Smart Ways to Enhance Cyber

Safeguarding future prosperity consequently mandates the adoption of certain measures, such as:

  • Identifying threats, recognizing perimeters, pre-empting attacks – internal and external.

  • Resisting attacks by upgrading security protocols and patches regularly across enterprise networks.

  • Responding with a comprehensive contingency plan detailing the role of their employees, steps for data retrieval and process of disclosing the attack.

  • Adapting to a changing business landscape by becoming tech savvy.

  • Harnessing emergent technologies and rope in automation and analytics to build robust defenses.

Digitization drives growth but also attracts its fair share of risks. Most of these surround a valuable asset – information. By creating a roadmap for developing cyber resilience and investing in state of the art IT security infrastructure, organizations can gain ground on hackers, innovate and expand, leverage customers’ trust and beget the competitive advantage that can set them apart. While this may not eliminate the prospect of breaches totally, it will make defenses more comprehensive and assessment of cyber threats, accurate.
